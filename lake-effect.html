LAKE EFFECT

A Prohibition-Era Crime Novel
AARON CHRISTOPHER
// THE BOOK

Chicago, 1926. Jack Becker is a mechanic with a talent for making cars do things their manufacturers never intended. When the job that was supposed to be temporary becomes something he can't walk away from—and the woman he can't stop thinking about turns out to be his oldest friend's sister—Jack finds himself holding the only thing more dangerous than a secret: a reason to stay alive.

A Prohibition-era crime novel that begins in the steamy heat of a Chicago summer and ends in the lake-effect snow of upstate New York. Lake Effect is the story of loyalty, leverage, and the distance between who you were and who you're trying to become.
